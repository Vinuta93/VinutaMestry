Save The Art

Save the Art is an NGO which has undertaken a social initiative towards preservation of the valuable and ancient art forms which are gradually vanishing and are on the verge of being totally extinct.

Project goal: We intend to re-inculcate the value and importance of Indiaâ€™s rich cultural heritage that is strongly reflected by its ancient art forms.

Creative solution: This motive can be achieved only through invoking a strong sense of realisation among the people about the richness, beauty and depth of these gradually vanishing art forms. The logo of Save the Art is a very typical and a decade old symbol that provides a very strong public recognition and appreciation towards the ancient Indian art forms and the talented artists. The Red Dot has been used for decades in the galleries to indicate a piece of art which has already been sold and is no longer available for sale, while still leaving it on view as a reminder as well as adding more value and importance to that particular piece of art. The logo stipulates how a particular art work stands out as compared to others and serves as a mark of instant public recognition for all such artists who deserve to be appreciated for their valuable and distinct skills and talent. Since most of the ancient Indian art forms are not valued and recognised to a considerable extent, the Red Dot in the logo reflects a slightly unfinished rounded symbol.
